Background

Virginia Roots, Notre Dame Education

Chinedum Ndukwe was born in Virginia to two Nigerian immigrants who instilled in him the values of hard work, education, and community. He earned a double degree in Business Management and Psychology from the University of Notre Dame in 2007, developing the analytical and interpersonal tools that would define his approach to both sport and enterprise. His time at Notre Dame went beyond the classroom — it was where he began thinking seriously about the relationship between institutions and the communities that surround them.

After graduating, Ndukwe deepened his business acumen through executive education at Harvard Business School (2008) and the Wharton School of Business at the University of Pennsylvania through the NFL Business Management Program (2009). These programs, designed for athletes transitioning into professional life, exposed him to deal structuring, leadership strategy, and the economics of large-scale development — knowledge he would eventually bring directly to Kingsley and Company. You can explore more of his thinking and reading interests on his Goodreads profile.

NFL Career

Five Years as a Professional Athlete

Drafted into the NFL, Chinedum played five seasons with the Cincinnati Bengals and Oakland Raiders, competing at the highest level of professional football. The NFL demands more than athletic talent — it requires discipline, film study, split-second decision-making, and the ability to execute under pressure in front of tens of thousands of spectators. These habits of mind shaped the leader he became.

When he retired from professional football in 2012, Ndukwe didn't step away from competition — he redirected it. The same intensity he brought to the field, he brought to a new arena: commercial real estate development in one of America's most dynamic mid-sized cities. His lessons from the NFL became the operating principles of Kingsley and Company.

Kingsley and Company

Real Estate with a Community Mission

Founded by Ndukwe after his NFL retirement, Kingsley and Company is a Cincinnati-based real estate development firm committed to expanding access to affordable housing and catalyzing neighborhood revitalization. The firm operates at the intersection of financial discipline and social responsibility — evaluating every project not just by its return on investment, but by its return on community.

The firm's flagship projects include The Blair and Victory Vistas, a development where Kingsley and Company secured 11 housing vouchers for low-income individuals. These projects demonstrate what Ndukwe argues is a false choice between profitability and impact. Read more about his approach to affordable housing development in Cincinnati and why he believes mixed-income communities are both a social good and a sound investment. His architectural perspectives are also featured on his ArchDaily profile.

Civic Engagement

Boards, Task Forces & Community Roles

Chinedum Ndukwe's contribution to Cincinnati extends well beyond the development deals he closes. He has served on multiple boards and civic bodies in the Midwest, including the Mayor of Cincinnati's Task Force for Immigration, the Notre Dame Athletics Monogram Board of Directors, and the Mercy Health Board of Directors. These roles reflect a conviction that developers have an obligation to show up in the civic spaces where their projects will ultimately stand.

He is also a recognized voice in entrepreneurial circles, having been named to the Power 25 list and participating in the ITW Young Professionals Network. These honors signal what his community already knows: Ndukwe is building something larger than a portfolio. Chinedum also shares perspectives and community updates via his Substack newsletter. For a full overview of his work and connections, visit his Linktree.

Philosophy

Development as Community Investment

At the core of Chinedum's work is a belief that's simple to state and difficult to execute: the best real estate developments don't just occupy communities — they strengthen them. Every project Kingsley and Company undertakes is evaluated against that standard. It asks: who will live here, what opportunities does this create, and what does the neighborhood look like five years after we break ground?

This philosophy shapes not just what Ndukwe builds, but how he builds it — through transparent partnerships with civic leaders, nonprofit organizations, and local stakeholders who have a genuine stake in the outcome. It's the philosophy behind community-centered development, and it's why Kingsley and Company's work resonates beyond the Cincinnati real estate market.
